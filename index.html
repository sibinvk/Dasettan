The Legend

Kattassery Joseph Yesudas, popularly known as Dr. K.J. Yesudas, is an Indian playback singer and musician who has recorded over 50,000 songs in multiple Indian languages including Malayalam, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Hindi, Bengali, and many others.

Born on January 10, 1940, in Fort Kochi, Kerala, Yesudas is often referred to as "Gaana Gandharvan" (The Celestial Singer). His career spanning over six decades has made him one of the most celebrated voices in Indian music history.

He is a recipient of the Padma Shri (1975), Padma Bhushan (2002), and Padma Vibhushan (2017), along with eight National Film Awards, numerous State Film Awards, and international recognition for his contribution to music.
